Welcome! I am a PhD Candidate in Political Science at the University of Pittsburgh. 

My research examines the conditions under which incumbents are punished but not removed in the aftermath of economic shocks. In my dissertation, I study how electoral rules and the structure of competition, including the fragmentation of the opposition vote and the presence of viable alternatives, shape whether withdrawn support produces meaningful changes in who governs. I use causal inference methods to identify both the effect of these shocks and the mechanisms behind it.

I hold a BA in Social Sciences from the Federal University of Minas Gerais, Brazil (2022) and an MA in Political Science from the University of Pittsburgh (2024).
